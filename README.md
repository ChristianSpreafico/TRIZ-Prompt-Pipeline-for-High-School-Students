# TRIZ Prompt Pipeline for High School Students

An educational collection of AI prompts designed to introduce **TRIZ (Theory of Inventive Problem Solving)** to high school students through a guided, step-by-step learning experience.

---

## About the Project

This project was developed by **Edoardo Rondi**, student at **Liceo ...**, under the supervision of **Christian Spreafico** and **Daniele Landi** from the **University of Bergamo (Italy)**.

The objective of this repository is to make the TRIZ methodology accessible to younger learners by combining its systematic innovation principles with the interactive capabilities of modern Large Language Models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT.  

---

## What is TRIZ?

**TRIZ (Theory of Inventive Problem Solving)** is a systematic methodology for innovation developed by the Soviet engineer **Genrich Altshuller**. It provides structured tools to analyze technical problems, identify contradictions, and generate creative and effective solutions based on recurring patterns found in successful inventions.

Today, TRIZ is widely applied in engineering, product design, education, and innovation management.

---

## Repository Purpose

This repository provides a sequence of carefully designed prompts that guide students through the main stages of the TRIZ methodology.

Each prompt focuses on a specific step of the problem-solving process and builds upon the information collected during the previous steps, creating a complete learning pipeline.

The prompts are intended for:

- High school students
- STEM teachers
- Innovation workshops
- Educational laboratories
- Anyone interested in learning TRIZ with AI assistance

## How to Use the Prompt Pipeline

The prompts are designed to be used **sequentially**.

1. Open the first prompt (`01_Problem_Definition.md`).
2. Copy its content into your preferred AI assistant (e.g., ChatGPT).
3. Answer the questions provided by the AI.
4. Continue with the next prompt in numerical order.
5. Repeat the process until the entire TRIZ workflow is completed.

Each prompt stores the information collected during the previous steps, allowing the AI to progressively build a complete TRIZ analysis of the problem.

---

## Educational Goal

Rather than providing immediate solutions, this prompt pipeline encourages students to develop analytical thinking, understand the principles of systematic innovation, and learn how to approach complex problems using the TRIZ methodology.

The repository is intended as an educational resource that supports both classroom activities and independent learning.
