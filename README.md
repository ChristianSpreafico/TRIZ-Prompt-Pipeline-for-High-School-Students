# TRIZ Prompt Pipeline for High School Students

An educational collection of AI prompts designed to introduce **TRIZ (Theory of Inventive Problem Solving)** to high school students through a guided, step-by-step learning experience.

---

# About the Project

This project was developed by **Edoardo Rondi**, student at **Liceo Scientifico Statale Lorenzo Mascheroni**, under the supervision of **Christian Spreafico** and **Daniele Landi** from the **University of Bergamo (Italy)**.

The objective of this repository is to make the TRIZ methodology accessible to younger learners by combining its systematic innovation principles with the interactive capabilities of modern Large Language Models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT.

Rather than simply generating solutions, the prompt pipeline guides students through the same reasoning process followed by engineers and innovation professionals when solving technical problems. The AI acts as an interactive tutor, encouraging structured thinking instead of providing immediate answers.

# What is TRIZ?

**TRIZ (Theory of Inventive Problem Solving)** is a systematic methodology for innovation developed by the Soviet engineer **Genrich Altshuller** after analyzing hundreds of thousands of patents.

Altshuller observed that innovative inventions rarely emerged from random inspiration. Instead, they followed recurring patterns and solved similar types of contradictions across different engineering fields.

Based on these observations, TRIZ provides a collection of analytical tools that help engineers transform complex problems into structured models and systematically generate innovative solutions.

Unlike traditional brainstorming, TRIZ does not rely primarily on creativity or intuition. Instead, it offers a logical and repeatable problem-solving process based on engineering knowledge accumulated over decades of technological evolution.

Today, TRIZ is widely used in:

- Mechanical Engineering
- Product Design
- Manufacturing
- Aerospace
- Automotive Engineering
- Medical Devices
- Artificial Intelligence
- Innovation Management
- Education

# Fundamental Concepts of TRIZ

The prompt pipeline introduces students to several fundamental TRIZ concepts, including:

### Technical Systems

Every product or process can be described as a **Technical System**, composed of elements interacting to perform one or more useful functions.

Examples include:

- Hairdryer
- Bicycle
- Coffee Machine
- Washing Machine
- School Backpack
- Smartphone

---

### Requirements

A Technical System exists because it satisfies one or more **requirements**.

Examples:

- Dry hair quickly.
- Wash clothes effectively.
- Transport a student comfortably.
- Clean contaminated fabrics.

Understanding the real requirements is the first step toward identifying opportunities for innovation.

---

### Contradictions

One of the central ideas of TRIZ is that engineering problems often arise because improving one characteristic causes another characteristic to worsen.

For example:

- Increasing cleaning effectiveness increases water consumption.
- Increasing strength increases weight.
- Increasing speed reduces precision.

TRIZ calls these situations **technical contradictions**.

In more advanced situations, the same component may need opposite properties at the same time (for example, being both rigid and flexible). These are called **physical contradictions**.

Resolving contradictions without compromise is one of the main objectives of TRIZ.

---

### Resources

TRIZ encourages engineers to exploit **existing resources** before introducing new components.

Resources may include:

- Materials
- Energy
- Space
- Time
- Information
- Environmental conditions
- Existing system components

Using available resources generally leads to simpler, cheaper, and more efficient solutions.

---

### Ideality

One of the key concepts of TRIZ is the **Ideal Final Result (IFR)**.

An ideal system provides all useful functions while minimizing:

- Cost
- Energy consumption
- Material usage
- Complexity
- Harmful effects

Although perfectly ideal systems rarely exist, thinking in terms of ideality helps engineers identify promising directions for innovation.

---

### Inventive Principles

TRIZ includes **40 Inventive Principles**, which are general solution strategies extracted from successful inventions.

Examples include:

- Segmentation
- Dynamization
- Prior Action
- Another Dimension
- Feedback
- Universality

These principles help transform abstract contradictions into practical engineering solutions.

# How the Prompt Pipeline Works

The repository is organized as a progressive learning workflow.

Each prompt represents one stage of the TRIZ methodology and has a specific objective.

The AI asks the student a series of questions, analyzes the answers, and progressively builds a complete model of the technical problem.

The output produced by one prompt becomes the starting point for the next one, allowing the entire analysis to evolve step by step.

This modular structure makes the methodology easier to understand while avoiding information overload.

---

# How to Use the Prompt Pipeline

The prompts are designed to be used **sequentially**.

1. Open the first prompt (`01_Problem_Definition.md`).
2. Copy its content into your preferred AI assistant (e.g., ChatGPT).
3. Answer the questions provided by the AI.
4. Continue with the next prompt in numerical order.
5. Repeat the process until the entire TRIZ workflow is completed.

Each prompt stores the information collected during the previous steps, allowing the AI to progressively build a complete TRIZ analysis of the problem.

Students are encouraged to answer carefully and consistently, since each stage depends on the information generated in the previous ones.

# Educational Goals

The primary objective of this repository is educational rather than computational.

Students are encouraged to:

- Understand how engineering problems can be modeled systematically.
- Learn how to identify useful and harmful functions.
- Recognize technical and physical contradictions.
- Explore the concept of ideality.
- Discover how innovation can emerge from structured reasoning.
- Develop analytical and critical thinking skills.
- Experience AI as a learning companion rather than as a simple answer generator.

The repository can be used both in classroom activities and for independent learning.

---

# Why Use AI with TRIZ?

Large Language Models can significantly improve the learning experience by acting as interactive tutors.

Instead of replacing the student, the AI:

- asks guiding questions,
- explains TRIZ concepts,
- helps organize information,
- generates examples,
- supports reasoning,
- assists during concept generation.

The combination of TRIZ and AI creates a highly interactive educational environment where students actively construct knowledge instead of passively reading theory.
